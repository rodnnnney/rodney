Memecoins are fundamentally flawed. You might think I'm saying this because I've only lost money trading them and haven't achieved the promised generational wealth - that's a fair assumption, but there's more to it than that.

If you take a step back and look at memes for what they are, you'll notice that they undermine the credibility of cryptocurrency as a whole. In this short essay, I'll explain my perspective on memecoins and why I believe they're detrimental to the crypto ecosystem.

When the Bitcoin whitepaper was released in 2008, Satoshi envisioned Bitcoin as a solution to the double-spending problem, centralization issues, and as a means for peer-to-peer transactions without the need for central authorities.

Why Memes Hurt Crypto

I fundamentally dislike memecoins because they are zero-sum games. For someone to achieve a 10x return and "retire their parents," that profit must come from somewhere else (i.e., someone else's losses). This dynamic is inherently extractive and predatory. It's disappointing that while blockchain and Web3 are groundbreaking technologies, one of their most prominent use cases has become speculation at others' expense.

Some argue that memecoins are similar to gambling - and with 24/7 markets, there are indeed parallels to online betting. However, when people gamble, they generally understand and accept the risks. With crypto, memecoins negatively impact the mainstream perception of the entire ecosystem. Unlike gambling, cryptocurrency technology can enable near-instant cross-border transactions, eliminate intermediaries, and leverage cryptography for security. Memecoins represent pure greed amplified by software, undermining the revolutionary potential of blockchain technology. Not to mention the obvious problems like snipers, rug pulls, and celebrities hyping up coins only to dump them on their fans. At the end of the day, crypto is just software - it is neither inherently good nor bad. Code has no feelings; it is the people who wield this technology that determine whether its impact is positive or negative.
